PointBox Console Help (Draft)

 

Floating the toolbars: Almost all tool bars in PB can be floated by clicking on the extreme left where the six dots are located and drag the bar away from its current position until its outline turns grey.

 

TALKER:

 

The talker is the module of pointbox which contains all active buddy chats. Buddy chats are contained in a buddy tab which can be opened by clicking on the buddy’s name in the buddy list.

 

 

Buddy Tab: A buddy tab is a tab in the talker which contains a chat with a buddy. It is created by double clicking on the buddy’s name in the buddy list.

 

Group Tab: A group tab is a special tab that can be created by double clicking on a groups name in the buddy list. Sending messages from this tab will send an identical message to each of the buddies in the group.

 

Adjusting the Divider: To adjust the position of the divider, position the mouse over it, click, hold, and move the mouse up and down. The dividers on all of the buddy tabs will have identical positions when the adjustments are finished.

 

Right Clicking: A right click anywhere on the talker will open a new menu that allows the user to access a number of functions

 

Display time stamp: an option to allow the talker to display a timestamp on each of the IMs in the current buddy tab.

 

Display price: an option to allow the talker to display a price for each of the quotes in the current buddy tab.

 

Colors and Fonts: the color and font of the user’s as well as the current buddy’s IMs can be customized on forms opened from the right click menu

 

Cut, Copy, Paste: the cut copy and paste functions can also be accessed by right clicking.

 

UPPER TOOLBAR:

 

Displaying the toolbar vertically: To display the tool bar in a vertical fashion click on the extreme left where the six dots are located and drag it to either the left or right hand side of the talker until its outline turns red. Note: if the lower toolbar is removed the upper one by be placed at the bottom of the talker.

 

Looking up a conversation with a buddy:

To look up a conversation with a buddy click the first button. The lookup message tab will appear. Use the checkboxes to select which buddylists you want to search, and enter your buddy’s name and click ok.

 

Floating a buddy tab: 

To float a buddy tab click the second button on the toolbar. The tab can now be moved around the screen independently of the talker.

 

 

Sorting buddy tabs: 

The third and fourth buttons on the toolbar can be used to quickly sort the buddy tabs alphabetically. The upward pointing arrow sorts the tabs in A to Z fashion while the downward facing arrow sorts the tabs in Z to A fashion.

 

Close all buddy tabs:

Clicking the fifth button on the toolbar will give you the option to simultaneously close all open buddy tabs.

 

LOWER TOOLBAR:

 

Displaying the toolbar vertically: To display the tool bar in a vertical fashion click on the extreme left where the six dots are located and drag it to either the left or right hand side of the talker until its outline turns red. Note: if the upper toolbar is removed the lower one by be placed at the top of the talker.

 

Refreshing prices: To refresh all prices in the talker press the “Refresh [$]” button.

 

Opening the quote template form: To access the quote template click the “Template” button.

 

Sending messages: To send messages either click the send button or press enter inside of the typing field.

 

Broadcasting: Broadcasting is a function that allows messages to be sent to multiple users at once. The broadcast form can be opened either by creating a group tab and clicking send or through the distribution list in the buddy list.

 

QUOTE TEMPLATE: The quote template form allows you to quickly create a quote and send it to your entire distribution list at once.

 

Using the template a quote message can be built automatically by entering data into the required fields for a quote with either one or two legs. The quote can then be broadcasted to one or more of the user’s distribution lists.

 

BUDDY LIST: The buddy list contains four tabs which list all of the user’s buddies. These tabs are PointBox, AIM, Yahoo, and Distribution.

 

Right Clicking: opens a menu with four options: add buddy/group, remove buddy/group, change buddy’s group, Buddy profile.

 

Buddy profile: The buddy profile allows the user to enter and save the buddy’s name and contact information.

 


Building a distribution list:

If you have not previously built a distribution list the first button on the toolbar will import all of your IM contacts into the distribution list. Once a list has been created the second, third, and fourth buttons can be used to add, modify, and delete a group respectively.

 


Sending a broadcast message from the distribution list:

Once a distribution list has been created, buddies and groups can be selected and unselected. The sixth button on the buddy toolbar will select or deselect all buddies at once. When the desired buddies and/or groups are selected clicking the fifth button will open the broadcast message form. This form can be used to send a message to all selected buddies simultaneously.

 

VIEWER:

 

 

 

The viewer displays quotes as well as provides access to most of the platform’s functionality. Messages are deposited into four default tabs. Outgoing Message: contains all messages sent by the user. All Quotes: contains all messages, incoming and outgoing, that the parser identifies as a quote. Incoming Messages: contains all messages that the user receives. All Messages: contains all messages, sent and received, by the user.

 

The quotes in the viewer can be organized by any of the fields listed in the headers by clicking ordered by clicking again. The headers can also be rearranged by clicking and dragging.

 

UPPER TOOLBAR:

 

 


The upper toolbar provides one click access to most of Point Box’s basic features.

 

Connecting to PB:

If you become disconnected from the PointBox server click the PB button to restore your connection. Clicking on Gateway on the menu bar also gives the user the ability to connect/disconnect from PB.

 

Logging in to AIM:

To log in to AIM click the AIM button on the toolbar. Clicking on Gateway on the menu bar also gives the user the ability to connect/disconnect from aim.

 

Logging in to Yahoo:

To log into Yahoo IM click the Y button on the toolbar. Clicking on Gateway on the menu bar also gives the user the ability to connect/disconnect from yahoo. When logging into yahoo the recommended server to use is scsc

 Filter:

To create a tab containing only quotes from the current session that match your filter criteria click the magnifying glass button on the toolbar and use the filter form. Using the filter as many new tabs as desired can be created. The filter can also be accessed by clicking on Viewer on the menu bar, and selecting “Lookup Today’s Quotes”.

 

 

Search:

To create a tab containing all historical that match your search criteria click on the binoculars button on the main toolbar and use the search form. Using the search function only a single tab can be created at a time. The filter can also be accessed by clicking on Viewer on the menu bar, and selecting “Search Historical Quotes”.

 

Opening the quote template form:

To access the quote template click the seventh button on the main toolbar.

 Floating vs. Docked window style:

In floating window style the viewer, talker, and buddy list are broken into separate components. In docked window style the three components are presented together. Swapping between the two is accomplished by clicking the eighth button on the main toolbar. The user can also choose between floating and docked window style by clicking on options on the menu bar.

 

Layout style:

In docked window style the talker and viewer can be viewed in either a horizontal or vertical layout. This can be done by clicking on the ninth button on the main tool bar. Note: if PB is currently in floating window mode changing the layout style will automatically cause it to change to docked window mode. The user can also choose between window layout styles by clicking on options on the menu bar.

Choose color palette:

Clicking color palette on the toolbar opens a window for selecting the font color on the viewer. The color choice window contains numerous ways to customize the font color. The color menu can also be accessed by clicking on settings on the menu bar.

 

Select font:

Clicking the button with two As on it allows for customization of the font on all viewer tabs. The font menu can also be accessed by clicking on settings on the menu bar.

Configuring Settings:

the settings menu can be opened by clicking button containing the image of a gear. The settings menu can also be accessed by clicking on settings on the menu bar and selecting either PB, aim, yahoo, or pricing settings.

 

Futures Curve:

Click FC to see PB’s futures curve. The futures curve can also be accessed by clicking pricing on the menu bar.

Volatility Surface:

Clicking the Vol button shows an ATM implied volatility curve as well as the implied volatility surface. The page also contains the futures curve as well as the price for an ATM straddle. The volatility surface can also be accessed by clicking pricing on the menu bar.

 

Interest Rate Curve:

Click the IR button to show the interest rate curve. The interest rate curve can also be accessed by clicking pricing on the menu bar.

 

Computing the price of an option strategy:

clicking the Comp Button will bring up a form to enter an option strategy and compute its price. The computation form can also be accessed by clicking pricing on the menu bar.

Strip pricing:

A form to price a calendar strip can be accessed by clicking the Strip Button or pricing on the main menu.

 Publish pricing data:

to publish pricing files click the button with the image of a pencil or pricing on the main menu.

Shutdown PB:

Clicking the button with a stop sign that contains an x will shut down the current session of PB. PB can also be shutdown by clicking Gateway on the main menu.

 

Help Menu: The help menu provides access to PB version data and documentation.
